Bio

Liron Reiter is a filmmaker and editor based in New York City, working across narrative features and documentaries. She edited the narrative feature Yellow Rose (2018), which won the Grand Jury Prize and Audience Award at the Asian Pacific Film Festival, the Grand Jury Prize and Audience Award at Urbanworld, and the Jury Prize at the Bentonville Film Festival. She previously worked on the documentary feature A Ballerina’s Tale, which premiered at the Tribeca Film Festival in 2015 and follows Misty Copeland’s rise as she seeks to become the first African American principal dancer in a national ballet company. Her recent work includes the true-crime documentary The Three Lives of David Wong, supported by the Bertha Foundation and Creative Capital, about a wrongly convicted, undocumented Chinese man and his twenty-year fight for justice. Liron is currently developing and editing narrative feature projects. Liron is a graduate of the University of Southern California School of Cinematic Arts. After working in production on commercials and studio films, she moved to New York in 2008 to focus on independent filmmaking.
